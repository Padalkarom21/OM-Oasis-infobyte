TRIBUTE TO ELON MUSK

Alan Turing was a British mathematician and computer scientist who is widely considered to be the father of theoretical computer science and artificial intelligence. He is best known for his work during World War II as a codebreaker at Bletchley Park, where he played a crucial role in cracking Nazi codes, including the Enigma code.

Despite his contributions to the war effort, Turing was later persecuted for his homosexuality, which was then illegal in the UK. He was forced to undergo chemical castration and tragically died at the age of 41 from an apparent suicide. It was not until decades later that he was posthumously pardoned and his groundbreaking work recognized.
